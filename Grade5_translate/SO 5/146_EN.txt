Andorra. Pyrenees. Andorra is a small country in the Pyrenees mountain range, 
on the border between Spain and France.
In Andorra, goods are cheaper than in neighboring countries.
This is why many tourists come here to shop.
The capital is Andorra Ia Vella.
Cyprus: Ancient ruins in Kourion, Cyprus.
Cyprus is an island in the eastern Mediterranean.
Cyprus is the third largest island in the Mediterranean after Sicily and Sardinia.
Many tourists go on charter trips to Cyprus.
Tourism is also the country's main source of income.
The capital is Nicosia.
Malta. The fortress in Valletta.
Malta is a small island kingdom in the middle of the Mediterranean Sea.
It is considered the southernmost country in Europe.
The country's capital is located on the main island of Malta and is called Valletta.
In the 16th century, it was home to crusaders who used the famous cross that later became known as the Maltese Cross.
Castle Monte Titano . San Marino.
San Marino is a small country located in the heart of Italy in the Apennines mountain range.
The country gets its main income from the sale of stamps, collectible coins, ceramics, wine and olive oil.
High on a mountain top is the country's capital, also called San Marino.
Vatican City. St. Peter's Basilica.
Vatican City is the smallest country in the world.
The country is located in the heart of the city of Rome in Italy.
Vatican City is home to the Pope, the head of the Catholic Church.
The Pope's own guard force is called the Swiss Guard.
This small country is home to the largest church in the world.
Vatican City is really just the Pope's own palace, the Vatican, and St. Peter's Basilica.
About 800 people live in Vatican City.
