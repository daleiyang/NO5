Factual text

Sometimes it is useful to compare things or phenomena with each other.

You probably already do this in school subjects, for example when you compare eras or when you compare living conditions in different countries.

But you also compare things in your everyday life, such as which game to buy or which phone subscription to choose. 

The list of things you want to compare is endless.

In this chapter, you will learn how comparative factual texts can be structured. 

You will read and write about different religions to see how they are similar and how they differ.

You will also host a consumer program and make a comparison between different goods.
