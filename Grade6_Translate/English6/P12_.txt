SUS - SAVE OUR SEED
SOS - 拯救我们的种子

Hi, William! I'm in Svalbard with my parents. 
嗨,威廉!我和父母在斯瓦尔巴群岛。 

We're on a guided hiking tour ... in polar bear country. 
我们正在北极熊出没的地方参加有导游的徒步旅行。 

Our guide has got a rifle. 
我们的导游有一支步枪。 

This morning he came into our cabin and he asked us to stay inside while he was trying to scare off a polar bear.
今天早上,他走进我们的小屋,让我们待在里面,而他则去吓跑北极熊。

Hope I'll survive. James
希望我能活下来。 詹姆斯

James and his parents are in the town of Longyearbyen.
詹姆斯和他的父母在朗伊尔城。

James: Hey Mum, many of my friends are on holiday in Spain or Portugal now, lying on the beach, eating pizza.
詹姆斯:妈妈,我的许多朋友现在都在西班牙或葡萄牙度假,躺在沙滩上吃披萨。

Mum: Hmm ... we're in Svalbard to see a fantastic landscape, and we also have friends here. 
妈妈:嗯……我们在斯瓦尔巴群岛欣赏奇妙的风景,这里也有我们的朋友。

They work at the Svalbard Global Seed Vault.
他们在斯瓦尔巴全球种子库工作。

James: The same job you and Dad do at home in Wakehurst?
詹姆斯:你和爸爸在韦克赫斯特的家也是做同样的工作吗?

Mum: Yes, they also store seeds in a seed bank.
妈妈:是的,他们也在种子库中储存种子。

James: You mean like spare copies?
詹姆斯:你是说备份吗?

Mum: Exactly. It’s like a safety net against the loss of plant seeds.
妈妈:没错。这就像一个防止植物种子丢失的安全网。

James: Do you mean that crops like wheat, oats, rice and maize might disappear in the future?
詹姆斯:你的意思是小麦、燕麦、大米和玉米等农作物可能会在未来消失吗?

Mum: Yes, I do. We have to make sure we can go on growing crops even if there’s a dramatic change in the climate.
妈妈:是的。我们必须确保即使气候发生剧烈变化,我们也能继续种植农作物。

James: OK. I think we should start a seed bank on the moon.
詹姆斯:好吧。我认为我们应该在月球上建立一个种子库。

Mum: Actually, many scientists talk about that.
妈妈:事实上,许多科学家都在谈论这件事。

James: Lovely. I'm going to be an astronaut with seeds in my pockets to save the world.
詹姆斯:太好了。我要成为一名宇航员,口袋里装满种子,拯救世界。

Mum: Good idea! Oh, here comes Dad.
妈妈:好主意!哦,爸爸来了。

Dad: Hi there. I think I need a hot meal. I was inside the seed vault today. It was cold.
爸爸:你们好。我想吃顿热饭。我今天去了种子库,里面很冷。

James: Cool! Tell me about it!
詹姆斯:酷!说来听听!

Dad: The vault was built to last around 200 years, even if there are earthquakes or other disasters.
爸爸:种子库的设计寿命是200年左右,即使发生地震或其他灾难也能保持完好。

James: Why is it so cold?
詹姆斯:为什么这么冷?

Dad: Well, for the same reason we have a freezer at home. It’s to make sure nothing is wasted.
爸爸:嗯,原因和我们家冰箱的原因一样。这是为了确保不浪费任何东西。

James: Why do we need to keep all these seed brands?
詹姆斯:为什么我们要保留所有这些种子品牌?

Dad: Well, James, we don’t know today what type of seeds they can grow in 40 years from now.
爸爸:詹姆斯,我们今天不知道40年后这些种子能长成什么样子。

It’s important to have a backup of all these seeds because of climate change.
由于气候变化,保留这些种子很重要。

Mum: Yes, we must make sure that we'll be able to grow the food we need for generations to come.
妈妈:是的,我们必须确保子孙后代都能吃到粮食。

James: Thanks, guys! My children will have cereal and milk for breakfast thanks to you. Ha ha!
詹姆斯:谢谢你们!多亏了你们,我的孩子们才能吃上麦片和牛奶当早餐。哈哈!

Mum: Well, we're not alone, you know. There are around 1700 seed banks around the world.
妈妈:其实,我们并不是孤军奋战。全世界大约有1700家种子库。

James: Does that mean that there’s hope for mankind?
詹姆斯:这是否意味着人类还有希望?

Dad: It does, but it depends a lot on us.
爸爸:是的,但这很大程度上取决于我们。

James: Yes, we talk a lot about it at school. Anyway, who said something about a hot meal? I’m starving.
詹姆斯:是的,我们在学校经常讨论这个问题。话说回来,谁说要吃热饭了?我饿死了。
